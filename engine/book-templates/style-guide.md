## Grammar and Punctuation

### Oxford Comma
- [ ] Yes: "apples, pears, and plums"
- [ ] No: "apples, pears and plums"

- [ ] «Guillemets» for dialogue

- [ ] 'Single quotes' for quotes within quotes

## Formatting

### Headings

```
# Chapter Title
## Section
### Subsection
#### Minor section (rarely)
```

### Emphasis

- **Bold**: Key concepts at first introduction
- *Italic*: Foreign words, work titles, emphasis
- `Code`: Technical terms, commands

### Lists
- Bulleted: for unordered lists
- Numbered: for sequences, rankings

### Quotes

> Long quotes (more than 40 words) are formatted as blockquote.

Short quotes "remain in text".

---

## Genre Specifics

### For Non-Fiction

**Source Citations**:
- In-text: (Author, year)
- Footnotes: for additional comments
- Endnotes: for long explanations

**Examples and Cases**:
- Anonymize real people (unless public figures)
- Format: story → analysis → takeaway

### For Fiction

**Dialogue**:
```
"Hello," he said.
"Hello," she replied with a smile.
```

**Internal Monologue**:
- In italic: *Why am I doing this?*
- Or without emphasis, but clear from context

**Descriptions**:
- No more than 2-3 details at a time
- Through action, not static
- Sensory, not just visual
